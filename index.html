About

What entices one most about the copper craft of Kashmir is the sheer elegance it commands. A symbol of royalty, picked as if from the treasure box of elite charm. Beautifully carved, the copper ware sits high in the court of Kashmir Arts and Crafts, mesmerizing coppery bronze hue casting a spell of grandeur in the floral vines and other traditional styles. The use of copper for cooking ware in the households of Kashmir irrespective of differences in urban and rural lifestyles is as old as the advent of Islam here in the 14th century. And despite the onslaught of modern cookware, the glitter of copper utensils called Traam in local lexicon still outshines everything in the kitchens of the rich and the poor Kashmiri alike. The copperware containers designed under the project are made of 95% pure copper. The human body cannot synthesize copper on its own. Copper is essential for certain metabolic functions. It is the decathlete of metals—it’s ready to do almost anything you ask of it, effortlessly. They need daily care to prevent the buildup from the natural oxidation process of copper. They should be washed with a soft cloth and lime and salt or vinegar and salt, or a metal cleaning agent. Do not store any other liquid apart from water as it is unsafe to expose copper to other food acids. Use a soft cloth to clean your copper ware. Never use abrasive cleaning pads. You can also apply Mustard Oil for a regular shine.
Our team has introduced a new technique known as “Patina”, a term that refers to the blue-green layer of corrosion that develops on the surface of copper when it is exposed to sulfur and oxide compounds; to our new range of lifestyle products.
Kashmiri craftsmen are known for their prowess in expressions in paintings, employing their skill to making sublime textures in copper work. Originally, Kashmiri utensils were products made out of copper but lately there has been a steady replacement of the finer works of Kashmiri craftsmen with cheaper commercial alternatives. Hence, CTOK has taken the initiative to revive this craft
